1. Overview of Motorcycle Maintenance

As highly precise mechanical machines, motorcycles depend heavily on routine maintenance and timely repairs to preserve their performance and longevity. Acquiring foundational repair knowledge not only equips you with emergency self-rescue capabilities during long rides but also effectively extends your vehicle's lifespan and guarantees road safety.

Modern motorcycles consist of five core assemblies: the engine, transmission system, chassis/running gear, braking system, and electrical equipment. The golden rule of maintenance is always: "Prevention is better than cure."

2. Essential Periodic Maintenance

Based on mileage or time intervals, the following items are mandatory tasks to keep your motorcycle operating in peak condition:

Maintenance Item Recommended Interval Key Procedures & Focus
Engine Oil & Filter 2,000 - 5,000 km Drain and replace oil according to specified viscosity. Replace the oil filter to prevent debris from wearing out internal components.
Chain Drive System 500 - 1,000 km Clean off road grime, adjust the slack (maintain 20-30mm of free play), and apply dedicated motorcycle chain lubricant.
Braking System Pre-ride check Inspect brake pad thickness (ensure it is above 2mm), monitor fluid levels, and completely flush/replace brake fluid every 2 years.
Air Filter 5,000 - 10,000 km Clean or replace based on your riding environment. A clogged air intake reduces power and increases fuel consumption.
Spark Plug 10,000 km Check for carbon fouling, inspect the electrode gap, and replace if necessary to maintain optimal ignition efficiency.

3. Simple Troubleshooting Guide

Issue 1: Engine Cranks But Won't Start

Diagnostic Approach: First, ensure the kill switch is set to "Run" and the side-stand is up (for models with side-stand safety switches). Then check the three fundamentals: Fuel (Is there gas?), Air (Is intake clear?), and Spark (Is the spark plug firing?).

Issue 2: Handlebar Vibration or Pulling to One Side

Diagnostic Approach: Check front and rear tire pressures; look for uneven tire wear or bulges. Inspect the steering head bearings (triple tree) for looseness, and check if wheel balancing weights have fallen off.
